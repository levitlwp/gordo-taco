tacos from heaven
since 77

Gordo Taqueria (known by many as Gordo's) hasn't changed a bit since it first opened on Clement Street and 24th Avenue in 1977; and whether for price, taste or nostalgia, that's fine by its loyal-as-ever customers.

The Gordo's burrito is a staple of the Richmond District diet and has amassed a following throughout the Bay Area. Co-owners Manuel Hermandez and Dick Yamagami operate six locations-three in San Francisco, one in Albany and two in Berkeley.

The original Clement Street location looks just as it did in the '80s'. "Everything's almost exactly the same," Hernandez said. "No room to change much in there."

Kitchen included, the restaurant is about 300 square feet. The line is still long, the bulletin board still cluttered, and children still dangle their feet from the wood and leather stools.

And Hernandez liked it that way, "Maybe I'm wrong," he said. "I'm just old school."
